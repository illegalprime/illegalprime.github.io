Friends, friends of friends and Honored Guests,

Summer 2016 has come to an end. It was a humid, dry, hot, boring, titillating, relaxing, worldly and low-key summer. In the end, it was a summer like any other: heat waves came and went, days grew longer and then shorter, Ledecky fearlessly shattered records and the inevitable march of time carried on.

And yet, for each of us, Summer 2016 was a time to remember. For each and every one of us, this past summer was unlike any other.

We flew to faraway lands, drove home, climbed mountains and fell hopelessly in love. We saw family, met old friends and made new ones too. We fought, broke up, kissed and made up. We grew older and a little wiser, made mistakes, planted trees, painted schools and went to the office. We studied abroad and we studied a broad. We learned about ourselves and about the world.

And we will not let this summer go quietly into that good night. We will rage, rage against the dying of the light. We are the master of our fate. We are the captain of our soul.

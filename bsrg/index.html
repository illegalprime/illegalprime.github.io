Part 1: The Introduction

The following list of readings are articles or excerpts from larger works for those of you who may not feel like devoting hours out of each day to diving deep into understanding Socialism. This reading list is for people who may get the basic gist of why Capitalism is bad, but who may not be too familiar with Socialism as a theory. Much of these works deal with secondhand interpretations or explanations, so please make sure to explore more fundamental works further down below. With these readings, you’ll have to think deeply, but maybe not as long or hard as you would have to think when reading an entire book:
  1. Socialism: The Remedy for the Evils of Society — Peter H. Clark
  2. Socialism and the American Negro — W.E.B. DuBois
  3. The Negro and Organized Labor — Ben Fletcher
  4. Martin Luther King, Jr., as Democratic Socialist — Douglas Sturm
  5. Why Socialism? — Albert Einstein
  6. The Principles of Communism — Friedrich Engels
  7. Manifesto of the Communist Party — Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels
  8. Critique of the Gotha Programme — Karl Marx
  9. The Capitalist System — Mikhail Bakunin
  10. Marx’s Concept of Socialism — Erich Fromm
  11. The ‘Dictatorship of the Proletariat’ in Marx and Engels — Hal Draper
  12. State Capitalism and Dictatorship — Anton Pannekoek
  13. The Problem of Dictatorship — Rosa Luxemburg
  14. The Black Church and Marxism: What Do They Have to Say to Each Other? — James H. Cone
  15. How Capitalism and Racism Support Each Other — Richard Wolff
  16. The Ecological Crisis, Socialism, and a New Society — Murray Bookchin
  17. Letter from Prison — Shūsui Kōtoku
  18. Think Different — Robert Homan
  19. The Combahee River Collective Statement — Combahee River Collective
  20. Intercommunalism — Huey P. Newton
  21. Authoritarian Leftists: Kill the Cop in Your Head — Lorenzo Komb'oa Ervin
  22. An Insider's Take on How the Black Panther Party Was Hurt by Its Own Ideals — Don Cox
  23. Utopia, Not Futurism: Why Doing the Impossible Is the Most Rational Thing We Can Do — Murray Bookchin
  24. Let Your Motto Be Resistance: A Handbook on Organizing New Afrikan and Oppressed Communities for Self-Defense — Kali Akuno
  25. The State and Marxism — Mikhail Bakunin
  26. The Red Association (1870) — Mikhail Bakunin

— Black Socialists in America
